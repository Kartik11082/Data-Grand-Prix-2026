HMDA Story - Judge Explanation Script (Page-wise)
=================================================

Use this format for each graph while presenting:
1. What it is
2. How we did it
3. Why our approach works
4. What the data indicates
5. Technical query/formula


Page 1 - Overview
-----------------

Graph: Landing hero + chips
1. What it is:
This is our opening snapshot of the mortgage market: timeline, total scale, and the key shock metric.
2. How we did it:
We used yearly aggregates from Q1, found the strongest and weakest approval periods, and built the hero number and supporting chips from those values.
3. Why our approach works:
It gives judges immediate context (scale + direction) before they see deeper charts, so the narrative starts with evidence, not assumptions.
4. What the data indicates:
We processed about 155.1M applications, with a 2007 peak (21.8M), a 2008 origination floor (7.2M), and a sharp approval-rate gap of 13 percentage points between best and worst periods.
5. Technical query/formula:
- Base aggregate (Q1):
  - applications_y = COUNT(*) where action_taken != 6
  - originations_y = COUNT(*) where action_taken = 1
  - origination_rate_y = originations_y / applications_y
- Landing hero:
  - floor_year = argmin(origination_rate_y) for y in {2008,2009,2010}
  - peak_year = argmax(origination_rate_y) for y in all years
  - hero_metric = round(origination_rate_peak*100) - round(origination_rate_floor*100)
- Chips:
  - applications peak = argmax(applications_y)
  - originations floor = argmin(originations_y) in crisis window
  - years_to_recovery = first y > floor_year where origination_rate_y > floor_rate + 0.02


Page 2 - Collapse
-----------------

Graph 2.1: Gap area chart (2007-2010)
1. What it is:
It shows the gap between loan applications and actual originations during the crisis years.
2. How we did it:
From Q1, we counted applications and originations per year and plotted both series in millions.
3. Why our approach works:
Using both lines together makes market stress visible instantly: demand stayed high while successful lending dropped.
4. What the data indicates:
The biggest shock is volume: applications fell from 21.82M (2007) to 14.47M (2008), while originations fell from 10.44M to 7.18M in the same step.
5. Technical query/formula:
- Pseudo SQL:
  SELECT as_of_year,
         COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE action_taken != 6) AS applications,
         COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE action_taken = 1) AS originations,
         COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE action_taken = 3) AS denials
  FROM hmda
  WHERE as_of_year BETWEEN 2007 AND 2010
  GROUP BY as_of_year;
- approval_rate = originations / applications
- denial_rate = denials / applications
- display_millions = value / 1_000_000

Graph 2.2: Loan-type composition (2007-2010)
1. What it is:
It shows how the mix shifted from conventional loans toward government-backed loans during the downturn.
2. How we did it:
From Q2, we filtered to originated loans, calculated conventional share, and derived government-backed share as 100 minus conventional.
3. Why our approach works:
Percent composition removes scale noise and clearly highlights structural change in lender behavior.
4. What the data indicates:
Conventional share dropped from 94% (2007) to 74% (2009), while government-backed rose from 6% to 26%, showing a major underwriting regime shift.
5. Technical query/formula:
- Pseudo SQL:
  SELECT as_of_year,
         COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE action_taken=1 AND loan_type=1) AS conventional_n,
         COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE action_taken=1 AND loan_type IN (2,3,4)) AS govt_n,
         COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE action_taken=1) AS total_n
  FROM hmda
  WHERE as_of_year BETWEEN 2007 AND 2010
  GROUP BY as_of_year;
- conventional_pct = conventional_n / total_n * 100
- govt_backed_pct = govt_n / total_n * 100 = 100 - conventional_pct


Page 3 - Recovery
-----------------

Graph 3.1: Full-decade gap chart (2007-2017)
1. What it is:
It extends the collapse gap chart to show the full recovery path.
2. How we did it:
We reused Q1 yearly applications and originations across the full decade.
3. Why our approach works:
The same metric definition across all years enables fair trend comparison from shock to recovery.
4. What the data indicates:
There is partial recovery (for example 2012 rebound), but by 2017 both applications (12.2M) and originations (7.34M) are still below 2007 levels.
5. Technical query/formula:
- Same Q1 query as Page 2, but year range is 2007-2017.
- applications_m = applications / 1_000_000
- originations_m = originations / 1_000_000

Graph 3.2: Refinance wave chart (2010-2017)
1. What it is:
It shows how refinancing activity surged after policy and rate changes.
2. How we did it:
From Q3, we indexed yearly refinance volume to 2010 = 100.
3. Why our approach works:
Indexing normalizes raw counts and makes momentum easy to compare year by year.
4. What the data indicates:
Refinancing peaked at 133.9 in 2012 (+34% vs baseline), then cooled sharply, ending at 50.9 by 2017.
5. Technical query/formula:
- Pseudo SQL:
  SELECT as_of_year,
         COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE action_taken=1) AS total_originations,
         COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE action_taken=1 AND loan_purpose=3) AS refi_n
  FROM hmda
  WHERE as_of_year BETWEEN 2010 AND 2017
  GROUP BY as_of_year;
- baseline_refi = refi_n(2010)
- refiIndex_y = refi_n(y) / baseline_refi * 100

Graph 3.3: Structural loan-type shift (2007-2017)
1. What it is:
It tracks whether the market returned to pre-crisis loan structure or settled into a new normal.
2. How we did it:
From Q2, we plotted yearly conventional vs government-backed shares.
3. Why our approach works:
Share-based tracking shows persistent structural change, not just temporary volume swings.
4. What the data indicates:
Government-backed share moved from 6% (2007) to 26% (2017), meaning the pre-crisis structure did not fully return.
5. Technical query/formula:
- Use yearly Q2 results.
- govt_share_2007 = round(100 - conventional_pct_2007)
- govt_share_2017 = round(100 - conventional_pct_2017)


Page 4 - Behavior Shift
-----------------------

Graph 4.1: Lender mix (2007 vs 2017)
1. What it is:
It compares lender risk posture before and after the crisis.
2. How we did it:
We used Q2 shares for conventional/government-backed and paired them with era-level second-mortgage context.
3. Why our approach works:
A side-by-side era view makes regime change obvious in one glance.
4. What the data indicates:
Lenders became more conservative: conventional fell from 94% to 74%, government-backed rose from 6% to 26%, and second-mortgage share dropped from 9% to 3%.
5. Technical query/formula:
- conventional_pct_y from Q2
- govt_backed_pct_y = 100 - conventional_pct_y
- second_mortgage_pct is an era reference value in export layer (2007=9%, 2017=3%)

Graph 4.2: Borrower profile card (2007 vs 2017)
1. What it is:
It summarizes who the typical approved borrower looked like in each era.
2. How we did it:
From Q4 and Q3, we computed median income, approval rate, dominant purpose, and dominant received loan type.
3. Why our approach works:
It combines access, income, and intent into one comparable borrower story.
4. What the data indicates:
Typical borrower income increased ($76K to $83K), approval rate improved (48% to 60%), and dominant purpose shifted from refinancing (46%) to purchase (58%).
5. Technical query/formula:
- median_income_k = percentile_approx(applicant_income_000s, 0.5)
  where action_taken=1 and income valid
- approval_rate = COUNT(action_taken=1) / COUNT(*)
- dominant_purpose = mode(loan_purpose) among originated loans
- dominant_purpose_pct = dominant_purpose_count / total_originations * 100
- dominant_loan_type = mode(loan_type) among originated loans

Graph 4.3: Geography choropleth
1. What it is:
It maps state-level lending concentration (2007) and recovery strength (2017).
2. How we did it:
From Q5, we computed state volumes and recovery index (2017 volume divided by 2007 volume), then bucketed all states into low/medium/high using distribution-based terciles.
3. Why our approach works:
Using all states with consistent bucketing avoids cherry-picking and gives a complete national pattern.
4. What the data indicates:
Most states are still below 2007 volume by 2017; strongest states are near baseline (for example CO at -1%), while laggards like CT and NJ are around -47%.
5. Technical query/formula:
- Pseudo SQL:
  SELECT as_of_year, state_code,
         COUNT(*) FILTER (WHERE action_taken=1) AS vol
  FROM hmda
  WHERE as_of_year IN (2007, 2017)
  GROUP BY as_of_year, state_code;
- recovery_index_state = vol_2017_state / vol_2007_state
- map_value_2017 = round(recovery_index_state * 100) + "%"
- low_cut = quantile(recovery_index_all_states, 0.33)
- high_cut = quantile(recovery_index_all_states, 0.67)
- bucket = low/medium/high using these cuts

Graph 4.4: Top/Bottom state leaderboard
1. What it is:
It calls out the strongest and weakest states for fast interpretation.
2. How we did it:
We sorted the same state metric used in the map and selected top 3 and bottom 3.
3. Why our approach works:
It keeps ranking logic fully aligned with the map, so the story is consistent across visuals.
4. What the data indicates:
The top group (CO, ND, MN) is only slightly below baseline, while the bottom group (NM, CT, NJ) remains deeply below baseline.
5. Technical query/formula:
- Sort by recovery_index_state descending
- top_states = first 3
- bottom_states = last 3
- display_delta_pct = round((recovery_index_state - 1) * 100)


Page 5 - Summary
----------------

Graph 5.1: Crisis summary card
1. What it is:
It compresses the core crisis impact into one headline with a mini trend.
2. How we did it:
From Q1, we identified peak vs floor approval periods and plotted a sparkline of applications/originations.
3. Why our approach works:
It closes the loop between headline KPI and underlying time series.
4. What the data indicates:
The biggest breakdown happened around 2008, where applications were 14.5M and originations were 7.2M, reflecting a wide conversion gap.
5. Technical query/formula:
- floor_year = argmin(origination_rate_y) for y in {2008,2009,2010}
- peak_year = argmax(origination_rate_y) for all y
- gap_peak_pp = round(origination_rate_peak*100) - round(origination_rate_floor*100)
- sparkline uses yearly applications_m and originations_m

Graph 5.2: Structural shift donut/card
1. What it is:
It compares government-backed share in 2007 vs 2017.
2. How we did it:
From Q2, we derived government-backed share as 100 minus conventional for both anchor years.
3. Why our approach works:
Two-point structural comparison is simple, direct, and decision-friendly.
4. What the data indicates:
Government-backed participation rose from 6% to 26%, confirming a long-term structural shift, not just a short cycle.
5. Technical query/formula:
- govt_share_2007_pct = 100 - conventional_pct_2007
- govt_share_2017_pct = 100 - conventional_pct_2017

Graph 5.3: Recovery map summary card
1. What it is:
It shows which states recovered faster or slower by 2017.
2. How we did it:
From Q5, we computed recovery index and delta percentage, then bucketed all states and highlighted top/bottom performers.
3. Why our approach works:
It combines national coverage with clear leader/laggard signals, which is ideal for executive storytelling.
4. What the data indicates:
Recovery is uneven across the country: no top state is clearly above baseline, and several large states remain far below pre-crisis lending levels.
5. Technical query/formula:
- recovery_index_state = vol_2017_state / vol_2007_state
- delta_pct_state = round((recovery_index_state - 1) * 100)
- full-state bucket = terciles on recovery_index_state
- top/bottom labels from sorted delta_pct_state


One-line close for judges
-------------------------
All visuals come from one consistent pipeline (Q1-Q5), so every chart, KPI, and summary point is reproducible, comparable, and traceable back to the same data logic.
